Common Questions

Frequently Asked

Electrical work can feel complicated. Here are answers to what San Diego homeowners ask us most.

How do I know if I need a panel upgrade? +
Signs you may need a panel upgrade include: tripping breakers, flickering lights, a 100-amp panel (most homes now need 200 amps), wanting to add an EV charger or solar, or planning a major home addition. Most San Diego homes built before 1990 should be evaluated. We offer free assessments — just call 619-333-6470.
Do you pull permits, and why does that matter? +
Yes — we pull all required permits and coordinate inspections. Unpermitted electrical work can void your homeowner's insurance, create liability in a sale, and is a safety risk. In San Diego, panel upgrades and EV charger installs require permits. We handle all of it.
What's involved in an EV charger installation? +
A Level 2 EV charger requires a dedicated 240V/50A circuit from your panel to the charger location (usually the garage). We assess your panel capacity, run the circuit, install the charger, pull the permit, and coordinate SDG&E interconnection if required. The whole process typically takes one day. We also help with rebate paperwork.
Can you add an RV hookup to my property? +
Yes. We install dedicated 30A (NEMA TT-30) and 50A (NEMA 14-50) RV and trailer electrical hookups at residential properties throughout San Diego County. We run the circuit from your panel, install the correct weather-rated receptacle, and permit everything to NEC code.
Are you the same as other "Bradley Electric" companies I see online? +
No. SoCal Bradley Electric is an independent, locally owned company based in San Diego County, California. We have no affiliation with Bradley Electric companies in other states or regions. Our California Contractor License number is #1131979, which you can verify on the CSLB website at cslb.ca.gov.

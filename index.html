Understanding Complex Electrical Work

Learn more about common projects that require permits and professional installation

Why Upgrade Your Electrical Panel?

Many San Diego homes were built with 100-amp electrical panels, which are no longer sufficient for modern electrical demands. If you're experiencing frequent breaker trips, planning to add an EV charger, or upgrading major appliances, a 200-amp panel upgrade may be necessary.

What We Handle:
  • Load Calculation: We assess your current and future electrical needs to determine the right panel size
  • Permit Process: All panel upgrades in San Diego require electrical permits. We handle the entire permitting process with the City of San Diego
  • SDG&E Coordination: We coordinate with San Diego Gas & Electric for service upgrades and meter work
  • Code Compliance: Your upgrade will meet National Electrical Code (NEC) and California Electrical Code requirements
  • Inspection: We schedule and pass all required city inspections
The Process:
  1. Initial consultation and load assessment
  2. Permit application submitted to the city
  3. Coordination with SDG&E (if service upgrade needed)
  4. Installation day - typically completed in one day
  5. City inspection and approval
Good to Know: Panel upgrades can increase your home's value and may lower insurance premiums. They also future-proof your home for solar panels, EV chargers, and home additions.
Professional EV Charger Installation

Installing a Level 2 EV charger at your home provides faster charging (4-8 hours vs. 24+ hours) and lets you take advantage of off-peak electricity rates. In San Diego, EV charger installation requires permits and proper electrical planning.

What We Handle:
  • Electrical Assessment: We evaluate whether your current panel can support the charger or if an upgrade is needed
  • Permitting: Required for all Level 2 EV charger installations in San Diego - we handle all paperwork
  • Load Calculations: We ensure your electrical service won't be overloaded (must stay under 80% capacity)
  • Dedicated Circuit: We install a proper 240V dedicated circuit for your charger
  • Location Planning: Optimal placement considering distance from panel and vehicle parking
  • City Inspection: Final inspection to ensure code compliance
San Diego Requirements:
  • Electrical permit from City of San Diego
  • Single-line diagram showing your electrical system
  • Load calculations proving adequate capacity
  • Licensed C-10 electrical contractor (that's us!)
  • UL-listed EV charging equipment
Rebates & Incentives: San Diego residents may qualify for SDG&E rebates and state incentives. Ask us about current programs that can offset your installation costs.
When Are Permits Required?

According to San Diego Municipal Code, electrical permits are required for most electrical work, including:

  • Panel upgrades or replacements
  • EV charger installations
  • New circuits or significant wiring changes
  • Service upgrades (increasing amperage)
  • Major appliance installations requiring new wiring
What's Exempt?

Minor work like replacing outlets, switches, light fixtures, or circuit breakers typically doesn't require a permit - but it must still meet code requirements.

Why Permits Matter:
  • Safety: Ensures work meets minimum safety standards
  • Insurance: Unpermitted work can void homeowner's insurance claims
  • Resale Value: Proper permits protect your investment when selling
  • Code Compliance: Guarantees work meets NEC and California standards
We Handle the Paperwork:

As a licensed contractor, we pull permits, submit required documentation, coordinate inspections, and ensure everything passes. You don't need to navigate the permitting process - that's our job.

Important: Only licensed contractors or property owners can pull electrical permits in San Diego. Using an unlicensed electrician puts you at risk and may create issues when selling your home.
